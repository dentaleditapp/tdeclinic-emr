          üìå SECTION 1 ‚Äî GENERAL PRINCIPLES
          1.1 Medical History Review
          • Confirm diagnosis and level of control of the systemic disease.
          • List all medications (including OTC / herbal / homeopathic).
          • Ask about hospitalizations, surgeries, anesthesia issues and bleeding history.
          • Ask about drug allergies and adverse reactions.
          • Confirm last physician visit and whether patient has recent medical clearance (if relevant).
          1.2 Vital Signs
          • Record blood pressure, pulse and respiratory rate for all medically complex patients.
          • Random blood glucose (RBS) when there is diabetes / symptoms / concern.
          1.3 When to Postpone Elective Treatment

          Defer non-emergency dental care if any of the following apply:

          • Myocardial infarction < 30 days or unstable angina.
          • Severe dyspnea or decompensated heart failure.
          • BP ‚â• 180/110 mmHg on repeat measurement.
          • Active, untreated tuberculosis.
          • Warfarin: INR > 3.5 for planned oral surgery.
          • RBS > 300 mg/dL.
          • Severe anemia with Hb < 7 g/dL.
          1.4 Anxiety Reduction for High-Risk Patients
          • Short, morning appointments.
          • Explain procedure in simple language; avoid surprises.
          • Use music / relaxation; maintain eye contact and reassurance.
          • Consider premedication (e.g. short-acting benzodiazepine) only in consultation with physician.
          üìå SECTION 2 ‚Äî Condition-Specific Management (Reference)

          A. Ischemic Heart Disease / Angina / Post-MI
          • MI > 6 months ‚Üí elective treatment allowed with precautions.
          • MI < 6 months or unstable angina ‚Üí defer elective care, treat emergencies only with hospital support.
          • Pre-op: measure BP and pulse; confirm use of antiplatelet / anticoagulants; keep sublingual nitroglycerin at chair-side.
          • Local anesthesia: max epinephrine dose ‚âà 0.04 mg (about 2 cartridges of 1:100,000 lidocaine).
          • Avoid or minimize vasoconstrictor in poorly controlled ischemic disease.

          If chest pain occurs during treatment:

          • Stop procedure, sit patient upright.
          • Nitroglycerin 0.4 mg SL every 5 minutes, up to 3 doses.
          • Give oxygen 4‚Äì6 L/min if available.
          • No relief after 3 doses / 15 minutes ‚Üí suspect MI ‚Üí activate EMS / transfer.

          Analgesia:

          • Paracetamol preferred.
          • Avoid routine NSAIDs in patients on antiplatelets or with heart failure / renal disease.
          B. Hypertension
          • ‚â§160/100 mmHg ‚Üí routine treatment allowed.
          • 160‚Äì179 / 100‚Äì109 ‚Üí treat urgent needs only, monitor BP, limit epi.
          • ‚â•180/110 ‚Üí defer elective care, refer for medical optimization.
          • Avoid rapid changes in chair position; avoid long stressful appointments.
          • Limit epinephrine; avoid drug interactions with some antihypertensives and NSAIDs.
          C. Heart Valve Disease & Infective Endocarditis Risk

          High-risk cardiac conditions:

          • Prosthetic heart valves.
          • Previous infective endocarditis.
          • Unrepaired cyanotic congenital heart disease.
          • Repaired CHD with residual defects or within 6 months post-repair.
          • Cardiac transplant with valvular regurgitation.

          These may require antibiotic prophylaxis for procedures involving gingival manipulation / periapical tissue / oral mucosa perforation (see Section 3).

          Step 1 ‚Äì Identify Bleeding Risk

          • Anticoagulants: Warfarin, DOACs (Rivaroxaban, Apixaban, Dabigatran, Edoxaban).
          • Antiplatelets: Aspirin, Clopidogrel, Ticagrelor, Prasugrel (single or dual).
          • Inherited disorders: Hemophilia A/B, von Willebrand disease.
          • Acquired: liver disease / cirrhosis, thrombocytopenia, chemotherapy, etc.

          Local Hemostasis (All High-Risk Patients)
          • Atraumatic technique; remove granulation gently.
          • Gauze pressure 30‚Äì60 minutes.
          • Sutures (horizontal mattress preferred for sockets).
          • Local agents: Surgicel, Gelfoam, collagen sponge.

          • Uncontrolled bleeding despite measures ‚Üí hospital referral for systemic reversal / transfusion.

          • Prefer morning appointments after usual meal and diabetes medication.
          • Acceptable RBS: roughly 80‚Äì200 mg/dL.

          • Watch for hypoglycemia: sweating, tremor, confusion, tachycardia ‚Äì give oral glucose / juice promptly.
          • Minimize trauma; consider prophylactic antibiotics for poorly controlled patients undergoing invasive surgery.

          Asthma
          • Ask frequency of attacks, triggers and current medications.
          • Ensure inhaler is brought to the appointment.
          • Avoid NSAIDs and sulfite-containing local anesthetics in aspirin-sensitive asthma.
          COPD
          • Treat in semi-upright position; avoid supine for long periods.
          • Avoid high-flow O‚ÇÇ (>3 L/min) in known CO‚ÇÇ retainers unless medically indicated.

          Liver Disease
          • Check PT/INR in cirrhosis or advanced disease.
          • Use paracetamol cautiously (lower total daily dose).
          • Avoid NSAIDs due to bleeding and renal risk.
          Chronic Kidney Disease / Dialysis
          • Plan dental treatment for the day after dialysis (patient usually heparinized on dialysis day).
          • Adjust doses for renally excreted drugs.
          • Avoid nephrotoxic drugs (high-dose NSAIDs, some antibiotics).

          Hyperthyroidism
          • Uncontrolled hyperthyroidism ‚Üí high risk of thyroid storm ‚Üí defer elective care.
          • Avoid epinephrine in uncontrolled patients; limit dose even when controlled.
          Hypothyroidism
          • Increased sensitivity to sedatives and CNS depressants.
          • Short, stress-free appointments; avoid hypothermia.

          • Confirm diagnosis, medications and date of last seizure.
          • Avoid flashing lights or strong triggers where relevant.
          • Keep instrument field clear and avoid leaving instruments in mouth.
          • If seizure occurs: remove instruments, protect from injury, maintain airway, place in safe lateral position.

          • Avoid routine NSAIDs and aspirin where possible.
          • Treat in semi-upright position if reflux symptoms are significant.

          • Check psychotropic medications (lithium, antipsychotics, antidepressants).
          • Tricyclic antidepressants + epinephrine ‚Üí use minimal epi; avoid high concentration vasoconstrictors.
          • Many psych meds cause xerostomia ‚Üí emphasize caries prevention & fluoride.
          • Short, structured, supportive appointments.
          üìå SECTION 3 ‚Äî Antibiotic Prophylaxis for Infective Endocarditis

          3.1 Indications

          AP is recommended only in high-risk cardiac patients when the procedure involves:

          • Manipulation of gingival tissue.
          • Periapical region of teeth.
          • Perforation of oral mucosa.

          High-risk cardiac conditions (as above in 2.1C):

          • Prosthetic cardiac valves.
          • Previous infective endocarditis.
          • Certain congenital heart disease (unrepaired cyanotic, recent repair, residual defect).
          • Cardiac transplant with valvulopathy.
